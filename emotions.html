Emotions

Colors elicits various emotions and there are various study which back this. Multiple studies show that colors influence emotions, heart rate, and hormone levels.

Red

Red is associated with energy, power, passion,love, and danger. The brain shows a greater emotional and attention related response to red compared to other colors. Did you know that red has been proven to negatively impact cognitive tasks but positively impact physical performance? You also might have noticed that many brands use red. That is because of its ability to grab passerbys attention and evoke strong emotions. It's a versatile color and is associated with various emotions.

Orange

Orange is associated with enthusiam, creativity and energy; As its a color positioned between red and yellow on the color wheel. Its known for converying optimism, adventure and confidence. The color orange is primary used in safety contexts and spiritual settings as well as sports branding.

Yellow

Yellow is generally associated with positive emotions such as joy and happiness. The color is known for it's high visibility; which is why its often used for signs, vehicles, and clothing that relates to traffic and construction because they need to be easily spotted by others. Yellow signals caution, rather than danger.

Green

Green is associated with calmness, balance, and relaxation. Green itself symbolises growth, renewal, harmony, balance and much more. Green falls in the middle of the visible light spectrum, making green appear more vibrant to us compared to other colors. Since the color of nature is mostly green, green is used for camoflage and environmental branding. It can even be used in healthcare to promote a sense of calm and healing.

Blue

Blue is widely associated with serenity, relaxation, peace, stability, etc. But it can also be linked to sadness, depending on the hue, value, and saturation. The color blue is a popular choice in branding/marketing and it is often used for products and service related to hygiene, water, air, and technology. Studies show that the most common favourite colour globally is blue. This is because of the positive connections of the ocean and sky as well as its tranquil and secure feeling it gives off.

Purple

Purple is often associated with creativity, relaxation, calmness, luxury, and royalty. This color can stimulate imagination, creativity and help create a sense of balance. It also might help you connect with deeper thoughts. Purple is both calming and stimulating for the mind.
