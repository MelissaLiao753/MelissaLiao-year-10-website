Fundamentals

There are three fundamental aspects of colors; these are hue, value and intensity. These are what determines what color, how light/dark your color is how bright or dull it is.

Hue

Hues refer to its position on the colour wheel. Red, Orange, Pink, Purple, etc are all examples of hues. Some hues are actually darker than other colours because of how we distinguish colors and the property of the light itself. For example, yellow is lighter than blue due to how our cones and brain perceives them.

Value

Values refer to how light or dark the color is. As you add more white, the color becomes lighter and for black it becomes darker. This is where we get more pastel and dark colors. This is an extremely important point especially if you are looking to create contrast in your work.

Intensity

The intensity of a colour refers to how bright or dull a color is. You can desaturate a color by either adding white, black, grey, or the colors complement. Saturation/Intensity is important because it impacts visual depth and mood.
